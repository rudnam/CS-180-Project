Project Summary

This project involves analyzing a dataset derived from the Family Income and Expenditure Survey (FIES) in the Philippines using K-means clustering. The goal is to identify potential household classifications by leveraging the dataset's information. Our hypothesis suggests that these classifications will likely be associated with income levels and geographic regions.

Results

The team successfully performed K-means clustering on the dataset, resulting in the identification of 7 distinct clusters. Consistent with our expectations, these clusters were primarily determined by factors such as income, region, and expenditure patterns. Notably, clusters 4 and 5 effectively represent the upper and lower socioeconomic classes, respectively, while the remaining clusters fall in between.
